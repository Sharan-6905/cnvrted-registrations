## 1. What CNVRTED Is

CNVRTED is an AI-native revenue intelligence platform that tells B2B sales and RevOps teams **who is actively in-market right now** — not who merely fits a static profile.

Traditional lead databases (Apollo, ZoomInfo, Cognism) answer the question *"who fits my ICP?"* by handing over contact records: name, title, company, email. That answer doesn't change day to day. A company that fit your ICP six months ago still fits it today, even if nothing about their buying situation has changed.

CNVRTED answers a different question: *"who is showing real signs of buying intent, right now?"*

It does this by continuously reading public buying signals — hiring activity, technology changes, executive moves, funding events, product launches, and relevant conversations across platforms like LinkedIn, Reddit, and X — and matching those signals against a company's Ideal Customer Profile (ICP). The output isn't a static list. It's a living, prioritized feed of accounts that are showing intent *today*, each with the context explaining *why* they're worth contacting and *what* to say.

CNVRTED then closes the loop: once a signal is identified and scored, the platform can help draft context-aware outreach and sync the opportunity directly into the team's CRM — so the signal becomes a conversation, not just a notification.

## 2. The Category Shift

| | Lead Databases (Apollo, ZoomInfo, Cognism) | CNVRTED |
|---|---|---|
| **Core question** | Who fits my ICP? | Who is in-market right now? |
| **Data type** | Static firmographic/contact data | Live behavioral and intent signals |
| **Freshness** | Updated periodically | Continuously monitored |
| **Output** | A list to dial through | A prioritized, contextualized opportunity feed |
| **Outreach basis** | Volume — more contacts, more attempts | Timing — fewer contacts, right moment |
| **Underlying bet** | More data = more pipeline | Right signal at the right time = more pipeline |

CNVRTED does not compete on having *more contacts* than the incumbents. It competes on knowing *when* and *why* to reach a contact. This is the single most important distinction to protect across every page, headline, and visual on the site — every piece of messaging should reinforce **timing and intent**, not database size.

## 3. Core Capabilities (Full Platform Scope)

CNVRTED ships as one connected system, not a bundle of disconnected tools. At launch, the platform includes four capabilities that work in sequence:

### 3.1 Signal Detection
Continuous monitoring of public sources for buying-relevant activity:
- Hiring activity (new roles that imply new initiatives or budget)
- Technology and stack changes
- Funding announcements and company milestones
- Relevant public conversations and discussions (LinkedIn, Reddit, X)
- Company website and messaging changes
- Other publicly observable intent signals

This is the raw input layer: a continuous read of "what's happening" across the market.

### 3.2 ICP Matching & Scoring
Signals alone are noise without context. CNVRTED matches every detected signal against the user's defined ICP — industry, company size, role, tech stack, geography, and other firmographic and behavioral criteria — and scores the resulting match for relevance and urgency. This turns a firehose of public activity into a small, ranked set of accounts worth a rep's attention.

### 3.3 Contextual Outreach
Once an opportunity is surfaced, CNVRTED helps the rep act on it immediately, while the context is still fresh. The platform assists in drafting outreach that explicitly references the signal that triggered the alert — not a generic template, but a message grounded in *why this account, why now*.

### 3.4 CRM Sync & Enrichment
Surfaced opportunities, signal context, and enrichment data sync into the team's existing CRM and sales tools, so intent data lives where the team already works rather than in a separate tab no one checks.

**Documentation rule:** All four capabilities should be referenced as one continuous system — "signal → match → context → action" — not as four separate product features competing for attention. The homepage and product copy should always narrate this as a single flow.

## 4. Target Audience

### Primary: RevOps / GTM Operations Leaders
This is the primary buyer and the person the site's voice is written for. RevOps leaders are responsible for the systems, data quality, and process that make a sales org run. They are evaluated on pipeline efficiency, forecast accuracy, and the ROI of every tool in the GTM stack. They are skeptical of vendor hype, allergic to "AI-washing," and quick to ask "how does this actually work" rather than taking a claim at face value.

What they care about, in priority order:
1. **Signal quality and reliability** — is this real intent or noise dressed up as intelligence?
2. **Integration into existing workflow** — does this slot into the CRM and stack they already run, or does it become another silo?
3. **Defensible logic** — can they explain to their VP of Sales *why* an account was surfaced, in plain terms?
4. **Time-to-value** — how fast can the team see a usable, prioritized account list?
5. **Control** — can they tune the ICP definition and scoring rather than accept a black box?

### Secondary: Sales Leaders & SDR/BDR Managers
People who manage outbound motion day-to-day and feel the cost of low-intent, high-volume prospecting directly — wasted rep hours, low reply rates, team burnout from cold outreach that doesn't land. They care most about rep productivity and pipeline output, and they are the ones who will ultimately ask "does my team actually use this, daily?"

**Voice implication:** Write for a RevOps audience first. That means precision over excitement, mechanism over adjective, and a willingness to explain *how* something works rather than just asserting that it's powerful. Sales leader language (productivity, reply rates, rep time) can appear in supporting sections, but the primary narrative voice should sound like someone who has run a revenue operations function, not someone selling to one.

## 5. Stage & Proof Strategy

CNVRTED is **pre-launch**. There are no customer logos, no published case studies, and no quantified outcome metrics ("40% increase in reply rate") to put on the site. This is a real constraint, and the documentation system must account for it rather than paper over it.

**What this means for every other document:**
- **No fabricated or implied metrics.** No "(insert stat)" placeholders that could later be mistaken for real data, and no vague-but-confident claims ("teams see results fast") that imply proof which doesn't exist.
- **No fake logos, testimonials, or "trusted by" sections.** This is a hard rule, not a stylistic preference.
- **Proof comes from product logic, not social proof.** Because the site cannot lean on "look who uses us," it must lean on "look how this actually works." Mechanism transparency — clearly explaining the signal-to-action pipeline — becomes the primary trust-building device. A RevOps audience that can't see your customers will instead evaluate whether your *logic* holds up. The product walkthrough sections of the homepage carry more weight in this version of the site than they would for an established product.
- **The CTA economy reflects the stage.** Because there is no live product to trial and no customer base to reference, the entire site converges on one goal: **join the waitlist.** Every page, every section, every button should funnel toward this single action. There should be no competing CTAs (e.g., "book a demo" alongside "join waitlist") that dilute focus or imply a sales-led motion that doesn't yet exist.
- **Founder-led credibility, used sparingly.** Where appropriate, the site can lean on the clarity and conviction of the people building this — sharp thinking, a well-articulated POV on why intent-based selling is the future — rather than borrowed credibility from logos that don't exist yet. This should be subtle (a sharp "why now" narrative, a confident specificity in how the product is described) rather than an explicit "founder's letter" unless requested separately.

## 6. Conversion Goal

**Primary and only goal: Join the waitlist.**

There is no self-serve trial, no demo booking flow, and no pricing page at this stage. Every section of the homepage and every supporting page should be evaluated against one question: *does this make someone more likely to join the waitlist?* If a piece of content doesn't build understanding, trust, or urgency toward that single action, it doesn't belong on the site yet.

This has direct implications for `05-homepage.md` and `03-copywriting.md`: CTA copy must be specific and singular ("Join the waitlist," "Get early access") rather than generic ("Get started," "Learn more"), and there should be no secondary or tertiary CTA competing for attention.

## 7. Data Sources (Public-Facing Language)

At this stage, CNVRTED's public-facing language should describe data sources **generically and directionally**, not as a specific, enumerated integration list. Approved language includes:

- "Public conversations across platforms like LinkedIn, Reddit, and X"
- "Hiring and job posting activity"
- "Company websites and product pages"
- "Technology and stack changes"
- "Funding and company milestone announcements"

**Rule:** Do not name specific job boards, specific CRMs (beyond generic references to "your CRM" or, if needed for illustration, widely known examples like Salesforce/HubSpot used descriptively, not as confirmed integrations), or specific scraping methodologies. This keeps the site accurate to what's actually committed and avoids overpromising integrations that aren't built yet. As real integrations are confirmed, this section should be updated and the copywriting/homepage docs revised to match.

---

## 8. Pricing

**Pricing is not discussed on the marketing site.** No pricing page, no "starting at" language, no tier names, no "contact sales for pricing" — the model isn't finalized, and a pre-launch waitlist motion doesn't require justifying a price point. If a pricing section is requested later, it will need its own document and should not be assumed from this one.
